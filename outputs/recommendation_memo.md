# Recommendation Memo

## 1. Executive Summary

An A/B experiment was conducted to compare the existing onboarding experience (Control) with a new onboarding campaign (Treatment). The objective was to improve user activation and paid conversion.

## 2. North Star Metric

Paid Conversion Rate

## 3. KPI Tree Explanation

The North Star Metric is supported by Trial Start Rate, Onboarding Completion Rate, and User Engagement. Guardrail metrics include Refund Rate, Support Ticket Rate, and Days to Convert.

## 4. Experiment Result Summary

The Treatment group outperformed the Control group in:

* Paid Conversion Rate
* Trial Start Rate
* Onboarding Completion Rate
* Engagement Score
* Average Days to Convert

## 5. Hypothesis Test Interpretation

The Null Hypothesis was rejected because the Treatment group demonstrated a meaningful improvement in the primary business metric.

## 6. Guardrail Analysis

Refund Rate increased slightly but remained low. Support Ticket Rate increased and should be monitored. Faster conversion and stronger engagement indicate positive user adoption.

## 7. Segment-Level Insight

Performance improvements were observed across multiple user segments, including Region, Device Type, and Traffic Source.

## 8. Final Recommendation

**Launch the new onboarding campaign for all users.**

The Treatment group delivered stronger business outcomes while maintaining acceptable guardrail metrics. Continue monitoring support ticket volume after rollout.

## 9. Risks and Limitations

* Increased support ticket rate may require additional customer support resources.
* Long-term retention was not measured in this experiment.
* Additional monitoring after rollout is recommended.

## 10. Next Steps

* Launch the new onboarding experience.
* Continue monitoring guardrail metrics.
* Run follow-up experiments to optimize onboarding further.
* Measure long-term retention and customer lifetime value.
